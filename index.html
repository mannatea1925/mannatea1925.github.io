Ava Kaplan

Librarian / Scholar


Ava Kaplan is the Research & Instruction Librarian at Teachers College, Columbia University, where she is also completing an M.A. in Anthropology and Education. She holds an M.S. in Library and Information Science and an advanced certificate in Digital Humanities from the Pratt Institute. She is a Fellow at the Semantic Lab, working to represent archival materials as knowledge graphs. Before her current position, she was an adjunct librarian at Bronx Community College, a Summer Fellow at the Library of Congress, and a Fulbright Scholar.


Her research interests include critical information literacy, speculative archives, and the anthropology of knowledge production, libraries, and the internet. Her work uses methods such as  digital ethnography, qualitative analysis, and network analysis.
