Ava Kaplan
Librarian • Anthropologist
New York, NY

I'm currently the Research & Instruction Librarian at Teachers College, Columbia University, where I am also completing an M.A. in Anthropology and Education. I hold an M.S. in Library and Information Science and an advanced certificate in Digital Humanities from the Pratt Institute. I am also a Research Fellow at the Semantic Lab, working to represent archival materials as complex knowledge graphs. Some of my past roles include being an adjunct librarian at Bronx Community College, a Summer Fellow at the Library of Congress, and a Fulbright grantee.

Positioned at the intersection of information studies and cultural anthropology, my research explores critical literacies, speculative archives, and the anthropology of libraries and the internet. My methodological approach combines digital ethnography, qualitative analysis, and network analysis. In addition to my professional and academic work, I also am a reference volunteer with the Prison Library Support Network and member of the Council on Anthropology and Education.
